
## 2026-04-20

- https://discuss.google.dev/t/google-skills-arcade-2026-prize-counter-update/347189/48
- AI Summary:

[Yugali](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/1) announced a major change to the Google Skills Arcade program for 2026: instead of two prize counter openings per year, there will be a single, unified opening at the end of the year. This change is attributed to "persistent shipping constraints." The announcement also mentioned that the points required for prize tiers may increase and a new structure will be introduced to reward genuine learning progress.

The update was met with widespread disappointment and frustration from the community. A primary concern, voiced by users like [Greatest\_Analyzer](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/7), [Dragonight](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/8), [Quantorix\_Field](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/9), and [BCLAI](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/22), is the timing of the announcement, which came three months into the program. Participants feel it is unfair to change the rules after they have already invested significant time and effort based on the previous structure, leading to a loss of motivation for many ([Ricky\_Shahi1](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/13), [Amphion](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/18)).

Participants raised several critical questions and suggestions:

-   **Clarity on New Rules:** Many users urgently requested that the new prize tiers and point requirements be revealed as soon as possible to allow them to plan their learning strategy ([atulsharma3982](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/40), [stemxplore](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/41)).
-   **Point Expiry:** A major worry was that points earned early in the year would expire before the prize counter opens ([Sharmila\_Bhaduri](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/36)). However, a later post clarified that points will not expire ([Sharmila\_Bhaduri](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/45)).
-   **Program Integrity:** [BCLAI](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/23) suggested implementing stricter measures against users who complete labs with scripts or step-by-step guides without actual learning.
-   **Better Incentives:** [Saptarshi0](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/25) proposed reintroducing certification vouchers and leaderboards to foster healthy competition, while [BCLAI](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/22) suggested offering more practical tech-related rewards like external hard drives.

Several users also reported still waiting for swags from the previous season, citing a lack of communication from the shipping partner ([manaskhurana](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/26), [may\_phyu\_htun](https://discuss.google.dev/t/-/347189/35)). Overall, the community is asking for more transparency and fairness, particularly for those who started the program under the original set of expectations.
